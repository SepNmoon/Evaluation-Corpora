Park Street 110 . 30 Jan : 1787 Sir , it was with great pleasure yesterday , I received the favor of Yours of the 26 th : as it aſsured me of Honble Wilhelmina Murray s being well , and happily got over an event , that made all her friends anxious about her . Mrs. Dickenson , my Sister , and self ; joyn in compts of congratulation to You ; on the agreable occasion beg you will present the same to my Friend tell Her , I have seen the lock of Hair , of a Young Lady who we sincerely wish health and happineſs to I remain Sir Your much Obliged Humble Servant Captain Murray N.B . I flatter myself to hear in due time that Wilhelmina Murray grows fast , & means soon to walk & talk . --
