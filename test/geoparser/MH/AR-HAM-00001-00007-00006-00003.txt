Mr. Fisher presents his Complements to Miſs Hamilton , & begs her to accept his sincerest thanks for her kind note , & her obliging enquiries about the state of his Eyes . Mr. F . had felt himself much distreſsed to know in what manner he should expreſs his gratitude to her Majesty for being so gracious as to prescribe for him , but he was this morning releived from his anxiety on that account , as he had the good fortune to be honoured with a q uarter of an hours conversation with the Queen at Kew . Mr. F . had the pleasure of breakfasting with Lord & Lady Dartrey last week . Mr. F . 's friend & Collegue begs his Comps : to Miſs H . Kew . Oct : 22 . 1781
