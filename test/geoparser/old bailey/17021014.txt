THE PROCEEDINGS ON THE Queen 's Commission of the Peace , AND Oyer and Terminer and Goal-Delivery of Newgate , held for the CITY of London , and COUNTY of Middlesex , at Justice-Hall in the OLD-BAILEY . On Wednesday , and Thursday , being the 14th , and 15th , Days of October , 1702 . And in the First Year of her Majesty 's Reign . THE Sessions of Peace , of Oyer and Terminer , and Goal-delivery of Newgate , held at Justice-Hall in the Old-Baily , on Wednesday and Thursday , being the 14th , and 15th , Days of October , 1702 , before the Right Honourable Sir William Gore , Knt . Lord Mayor of the City of London , and Sir Salathiel Lovel , Kt . Recorder of the said City , with divers others of her Majesty 's Justices of the Peace , for the City of London , and County of Middlesex . The Jurors are as followeth . London Jury . William Christopher , Thomas Wharton , James Church , Benjamin Brand Nicholas Eale , James Bowton , Charles Bywater , Abell Ward , John Cleere , Thomas Harwell , Samuel Hall , Edward Adams . Middlesex Jury . William Kidgeel , Gent . John Johnson , Benjamin Noble , John East , John Tims , Robert Rogers , Thomas Bootes , John Wiburd , Robert Taper . Richard Bennet , Thomas Yeomans , Richard Beatnife . The Proceedings were as follows . William Stanton and Thomas Bell , of the Parish of St. Michael Queen-hith , were Indicted for feloniously stealing a Sculler-boat , value 50 s. from George Bignall , and two Sculls , value 3 s. from Nicholas Gilbert , on the 21st of September last . There was no Evidence that saw the Prisoners take the Boat from the place where it was lost , or the Sculls ; and the Prisoners alledging their Innocence , saying that they had been drinking at the five Bells in Wood-street with a Friend , and staying late , they came to Queen-hith and called for a Sculler , but none came ; at last they went over some Lighters , where they step 'd into the Boat and was taken . The Jury acquitted them . Sarah Morrison , of the Parish of St. Andrews Holborne , was Indicted for feloniously stealing 40 Yards of Taffety Ribbon , value 40 s. the Goods of Rachel Bonner , on the 7th of October last . The Evidence deposed that the Prisoner came into the Shop , pretending to buy some Ribbon , and took her Gloves , and put them cross her Arms , and leant on the Counter , and took up the Goods , and then went to the Door , and afterwards turned back , and held him in discourse , whilst she got another , and went backwards and forwards three several times , and every time took something , and at last went her ways ; but he let her go out of the Shop , and then took her with the Goods upon her ; she did not deny the Fact , saying , that she was in Drink and did not know what she did . The Jury found her Guilty . [ Branding . The first Evidence deposed , that she saw the Prisoner at the Door . and whilst she went to fetch a Pail of Water , the Prisoner went in and took the Goods ; but pursuing her , brought her back again , and the laid them down where some other Goods were . She denied the Fact , saying she knew nothing of it , but could not call any to her Reputation . The Jury found her Guilty . [ Branding . The Prosecutor deposed that the Prisoner was a Servant in the House , and took her opportunity to go away with the Goods ; but being afterwards takenand brought before the Justice , she confest the whole Matter . The Jury found her Guilty . [ Branding . See summary . ] Elizabeth Price , of the Parish of St. Dunstans in the West , was indicted for breaking the Dwelling-house of John Lang , in the Day time , no Person being therein , on the 7th of October last , about two in the Afternoon , and taking thence a Serge Counterpain , and other Goods of the said Lang . The first Evidence deposed that he had an occasion to go to a Gentlemans Chamber in the Temple , and did mistake the Stair-Case , and going up four pair of Stairs he saw a hole broke at three pair of Stairs , and looking saw a Woman peep , but did not see her Face , and mistrusting her to be a Thief , he went down Stairs , and spoke to a Gentleman in the lower Room , desiring him to stop her if she should come down whilst he went to call the Porter , and in the mean time the Prisoner came down one pair of Stairs , where she was taken ; upon which they went up Stairs , and looking in the hole found some Chissels ; but opening the Door found several Trunks and Boxes broke open , and the Goods pack 'd up ready to be carried away . The Prisoner denied the Fact , saying , she come to enquire for her Counsellor , and was told he lived in that Stair-Case ; she went up Stairs and was taken when she came down : All which Matters the Jury having considered , found her Guilty of Felony only , and acquitted her of breaking the House . Elizabeth Stephens , of the Parish of St. Martins in the Fields , was indicted for breaking the House of Daniel Saxton in the Day time , about 7 in the Afternoon , on the 14th of July last , no Person being therein , and taking thence a Stuff Wastcoat and a pair of Breeches of Rich . Davis , and a pair of Sheets and Blankets , the Goods of the said Saxton . The Evidence deposed that his Room was robbed and in pursuit he took the Prisoner in the Passage . She denied the Fact , and nothing being found upon her , the Jury acquitted her . Cicilla Gregory , of the Parish of St. Giles in the Fields , was indicted for feloniously stealing two Silver Spoons from Joseph Mathias , on the 10th of August last . The Prosecutor said that she came into his Chamber , and having a Mess with something in it , with a Siver Spoon , she tasted of it ; but being called down to the Door in the mean time , she took two Spoons and went her ways ; and said that afterwards he took her by Broomly-street in Mans Cloaths . The Prisoner denied the Fact , and there being nothing found upon her , and she calling divers Persons to her Reputation , the Jury acquitted her . Simon Johnson , of the Parish of St. Martins in the Fields , was indicted for Felony , for that he the 11th of June last , at the Fleet , in the Parish of St . Brides , London , did take to Wife one Susanna Cornwell , Spinster , and afterwards , at Knightsbridge , in the Parish of St. Martins in the Fields , did take to Wife one S - S - , Spinster , on the 23d of September following , his former Wife being alive . The first Evidence was the Chaplain of the Fleet , who deposed , that he did marry one Simon Johnson and Susanna Cornwell , and did believe that the Prisoner was the Man ; and the Clerk deposed the same . As to the second Marriage , the Prisoner confest the same , saying he would live and die with her ; but denied the first Wife , altho ' he had owned before that he was married to her ; but she having some mistrust that he was going to be married to a second Wife followed him to Knights-bridge in a Coach , where he was married a second time before she came ; and the Prisoner looking out of a Window and seeing his first Wife , ran away into Hyde Park , leaving his Hat behind him , never coming near his last Wife after . Another Evidence was the Minister of Knightsbridge , who deposed that the Prisoner and the last Wife came the 23d of September , about eight a Clock in the Morning , with a Licence , upon which he married them . Another Evidence was the Clerk , who deposed that after they were married he earnestly desired him to keep the matter secret . Another Evidence deposed that she heard the Prisoner own when he was in Custody , that he had two Wives , and that the first Wife told her she was with Child by him , and where they were married . Another Evidence deposed , that when he was going to Newgate he did confess that he had two Wives . The Prisoner for himself said , that he was not married to his first Wife . But both Marriages being so plainly proved , the Jury found him Guilty . [ Branding . See summary . ] Henry Dodds , of the Parish of Enfield , was indicted for feloniously stealing 2208 Beech Billets , and other Wood , from Thomas Barnet , on the 6th of January last . It appeared to be a malicious Prosecution . The Jury acquitted him , and the Court ordered him a Copy of the Indictment . Rachel Sampson , of the Parish of White-chappel , was indicted for a Burglary , for breaking the House of Susan Edwards in the Night time , on the 28 of September , and taking thence two Cheeses , a pair of Silk Gloves , and a pair of Worsted Stockings , and 7 s. in Money . It appeared that the Prisoner got in and hid her self till they were a Bed , and took the Goods , which she confest when taken . She denied the Fact upon her Trial , saying , she knew nothing of it . The Jury found her Guilty of Felony only , and acquitted her of the Burglary . [ Branding . See summary . ] Jane Housden alias Newstead , of the Parish of St. Andrew 's Holborn , was indicted for High Treason , for counterfeiting the current Coin of this Kingdom , on the 18th of August last . It appeared that there was an Information given , that the Prisoner followed the Trade of Coining , a Warrant was granted to apprehend her , and going in Holborn , by Turnstile , they met the Prisoner with false Money in her Hand , upon which she dropt upon her Knees , and would have thrust the Money into some Grates of a Window , but was prevented ; an other Evidence said , that she had bought several quantities of false Money of the Prisoner , who confest to her , that she had made it . The Prisoner was an Old Offender in this kind , and had nothing to say , only denied the Fact : The Jury found her Guilty . [ Death -respited for pregnancy . Joseph Day , of the Parish of St. Clements Danes , was indicted for privately stealing 3 pair of Crimson Worsted Stockings , out of the Shop of Anthony Huslam , on the 7th of September Last . The Prosecutor said he was fitting in the Shop with his Wife , and on a sudden she saw the Prisoner come from behind the Counter with something under his Arm , and immediately he followed him , and took the Goods upon him . He denied the Fact , saying he did not touch them ; but that did not avail him , the Jury found him Guilty to the value of 4 s. 10 d. [ Branding . See summary . ] Jane Bourne was indicted for a Misdemeanor for uttering false Money , she knowing it so to be . The Evidence deposed that she came into the Shop to buy some French Brandy , and offered a good Guinea to change , which was weighed ; but she pretending that she must not give so much for it , and desired her Money again ; at last she agreed to have it , and threw down a gilt Shilling instead of the Guinea , and they gave her her change out of it , and she went her way ; there was another deposed that she bought a Shift of her , and she gave her a gilt Six-pence instead of a half Guinea ; another said , that she came to buy 3 l. of Sugar , and she gave her a gilt Six-pence instead of a half Guinea . She having little to say for herself , the Jury found her Guilty . [ Fine . See summary . ] [ Pillory .
